Skin pigmentation, which refers to the color of your skin, is determined by the amount and type of melanin produced by melanocytes. Melanin, the pigment responsible for skin, hair, and eye color, comes in two forms: eumelanin (darker) and pheomelanin (lighter). Disorders of skin pigmentation can lead to hyperpigmentation (darker patches), hypopigmentation (lighter patches), or depigmentation (complete loss of pigment). These disorders can be caused by sun exposure, hormonal changes, genetics, skin damage, or certain medical conditions. Elaboration:
